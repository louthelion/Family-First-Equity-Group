Cash-flow and income projection education

Organize the numbers before relying on the projection.

Family First Equity Group can help organize basic property assumptions for education and review. We do not guarantee income, profit, ROI, rent, occupancy, cash flow, appreciation, or property value.

A projection is a planning scenario—not a promise.

A useful property review places possible income beside the costs and assumptions required to operate the property. Source documents and professional due diligence matter.

Numbers to organize

  • Rent potential
  • Short-term rental potential
  • Operating expenses
  • Maintenance and reserves
  • Taxes and insurance
  • Vacancy and management costs
  • Projected income

Build assumptions in layers.

Potential gross income

Start with the units or spaces, possible rent assumptions, lease terms, vacancy, concessions, and other potential income. Short-term rental scenarios may also consider nightly-rate assumptions, occupied nights, seasonality, cleaning fees, platform charges, and local restrictions. Estimates should be labeled clearly and supported where possible.

Operating expenses

Organize taxes, insurance, utilities, repairs, routine maintenance, landscaping, cleaning, administration, licensing, association charges, management, and replacement reserves. Historical numbers may not represent future costs.

Financing and owner-specific costs

Debt service, acquisition costs, capital improvements, depreciation, and taxes may significantly affect an owner’s result. Questions about loans, securities, taxes, or financial suitability belong with appropriately licensed professionals.

Stress testing

Consider what changes if occupancy is lower, repairs are higher, rents change, or a major replacement occurs. Multiple scenarios can make uncertainty visible; they cannot eliminate it.
