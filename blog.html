Short real estate notes

Practical property reminders.

Quick organization tips for commercial owners, families, short-term rental operators, and investors.

Property photos

Quick property photo tips

Use daylight, hold the camera level, and capture each exterior side, room, utility area, access point, and visible repair. Include context—not just flattering angles—and label when the photos were taken.

Submissions

What to organize before submitting a property

Gather the address, property type, size, units, use, occupancy, photos, known condition, income and expense records, owner goal, and preferred timing. Mark anything that is estimated.

Condition

Why property condition notes matter

A short list of known roof, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, structural, access, water, safety, and deferred-maintenance issues gives reviewers better context. Notes do not replace an inspection.

Guest use

Short-term rental readiness reminder

Before estimating revenue, consider use rules, insurance, safety, furnishings, cleaning, utilities, supplies, seasonality, maintenance, guest communication, management, and neighbor impact.

Investors

Investor document checklist

Ask for dated rent rolls, leases, operating statements, tax and insurance records, utility costs, repair history, service contracts, permits, and available condition reports. Verify material information independently.

Families

Family property organization tip

Keep the property facts, family goals, and professional questions in separate lists. That helps prevent assumptions about ownership or authority from being mistaken for legal conclusions.

Listings

Commercial listing preparation tip

Prepare accurate size, units, use, occupancy, condition, location, access, photos, status, and description. Avoid unsupported claims about value, income, zoning, or investment results.
