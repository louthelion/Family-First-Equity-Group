Learning Center/Photos, Videos, and Documents
Foundational lesson

This lesson offers a practical starting point for organizing property information and preparing informed questions.

Photograph in a consistent order

Capture the street, all exterior sides, site features, each room, major systems, visible damage, and identifying context with current, well-lit images.

Use video to show relationships

Slow walkthroughs can show layout and movement between spaces. Avoid fast pans, narration that guesses at defects, or filming people without permission.

Name and date files clearly

Use a predictable structure such as property-area-date. Keep original files and create a separate shareable set when needed.

Protect private information

Remove account numbers, identity documents, access codes, signatures, tenant information, and other sensitive data before sharing files.

Know when to bring in a professional

Property decisions may involve legal rights, taxes, title, financing, insurance, inspections, permits, valuation, or brokerage. Use qualified professionals for advice, verification, and decisions in those areas.
