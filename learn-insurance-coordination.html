Learning Center/Insurance Coordination
Foundational lesson

This lesson offers a practical starting point for organizing property information and preparing informed questions.

Create a property profile

Organize use, occupancy, construction details, major systems, condition, safety features, prior losses, tenants, and business activity accurately.

Report material changes

Renovation, vacancy, rental use, short-term rental activity, ownership changes, new entities, and commercial operations may affect coverage questions.

Compare more than premium

Ask a licensed professional about limits, deductibles, exclusions, replacement assumptions, liability, loss-of-income provisions, and required endorsements.

Keep evidence and contacts secure

Store policies, declarations, inventories, photos, receipts, claim contacts, and renewal dates where authorized decision-makers can find them.

Know when to bring in a professional

Property decisions may involve legal rights, taxes, title, financing, insurance, inspections, permits, valuation, or brokerage. Use qualified professionals for advice, verification, and decisions in those areas.
