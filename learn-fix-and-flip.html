Learning Center/Fix-and-Flip Basics
Foundational lesson

This lesson offers a practical starting point for organizing property information and preparing informed questions.

Write a realistic scope

List visible work by system and room, then reserve space for findings that may appear after professional inspection or opening walls.

Build a full project budget

Include acquisition, closing, design, permits, labor, materials, holding costs, insurance, utilities, financing, contingency, and disposition expenses.

Check rules before work begins

Zoning, permits, contractor licensing, safety rules, historic restrictions, and inspection requirements vary by location and project.

Treat resale value as an estimate

Comparable sales and market conditions can change. Use qualified local professionals and avoid treating a projected price or profit as guaranteed.

Know when to bring in a professional

Property decisions may involve legal rights, taxes, title, financing, insurance, inspections, permits, valuation, or brokerage. Use qualified professionals for advice, verification, and decisions in those areas.
